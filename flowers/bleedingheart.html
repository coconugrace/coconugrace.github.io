Lamprocapnos spectabilis

The bleeding heart originated from Siberia, northern China, as well as Japan and Korea.It is related to poppy family, Papaveraceae. It is a perennial plant, meaning it can live for over 2 years. The bleeding heart generally likes to grow in the shade. It blooms in the late spring- from about April to May.

The poppy is a relative to the bleeding heart.

To start, this flower originally earned its latin name, “dicentra spectabilis”. “Dicentra” generally meant “two spurs”, while the word, “spectabilis” was Latin for “outstanding, worthy to be seen. The name itself conveys the people’s view of this delicate flower. Japanese tale about the origin of this plant: A maiden rejects gifts (each gift representing a petal) on a prince, causing him to kill himself with a sword. In American and British culture, it represents true love. The root of this flower is sometimes used as a remedy to pain- either applied externally or as a liquid.

The bleeding heart has many species, and can come in the colors ranging from yellow, pink, red, or white.
