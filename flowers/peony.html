The peony is from Europe, Asia, and Western North America. is bloom season The peony is often marked by its grandiose display of colorful petals, as well as its fragrant smell. There are many species of peonies, blooming at different times of the year, and with colors varying from white, red, yellow, pink, etc. There are about 33 peony species. Most species are perennial plants, meaning that it can survive over two years. Peonies generally bloom late spring or early summer. These flowers can come in the form of a woody shrub, tree, etc. Peonies are popular to be grown in temperate regions (such as CT).

(Image source: Selena N.B.H) These are dried peony leaves that are to be used for peony tea.

This plant was one of my most vivid (young) childhood memories. I recall it being my favorite flower growing in our yard. Every spring, the flower would blossom great enormous flowers for a week, then fall to the ground in a heap of lush petals. Each peony season, my mom and I would rush to cut the flowers and send them to my teachers as a gift. It is surprising that this flower is not as well known as say, the rose. The peony is the Indianan state flower.

Peonies are often given as a gift to someone very dear.
